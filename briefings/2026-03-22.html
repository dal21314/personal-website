CHATGPT ATLAS BROWSER

Atlas continues its rollout as OpenAI's macOS-native AI browser. Recent updates include more persistent Agent Mode for repetitive tasks like bulk email processing, browser memories that now persist useful details across sessions, and multi-account support — allowing separate profiles for work, personal, and school use. OpenAI's stated goal is to merge ChatGPT, Codex, and Atlas into a single "superapp," with Greg Brockman and Fidji Simo leading the consolidation effort.
By default, OpenAI does not use browsing content to train models. Agent Mode remains in preview for Plus, Pro, and Business users. — OpenAI
Bottom line:
The coming superapp merger makes Atlas more important, not less — it's the browsing layer of OpenAI's long-term desktop strategy.

SECURE MESSAGING SPOTLIGHT

● SIGNAL
IBM + Signal Partner on Quantum-Safe Group Messaging
IBM Research has been collaborating with Signal's developers to redesign group messaging protocols to withstand future quantum attacks. Signal already upgraded its core protocol with SPQR in 2025, but protecting group membership metadata required a ground-up redesign. The new model makes group members — rather than the server — the gatekeepers of membership data, using NIST-standardized ML-DSA. Findings were presented at the Real-World Crypto conference this month. Implementation timeline has not been announced.
SOURCE // IBM Research READ →
● SIGNAL
Signal Status: Stable
No major outages or controversial policy changes this week. Signal continues to be the gold standard for open-source E2E encrypted messaging for mainstream users.

● THREEMA
IBM + Threema Partner on Quantum-Safe Encryption
Alongside the Signal collaboration, Threema is working with IBM Research to integrate ML-KEM — NIST's standardized post-quantum key encapsulation mechanism — into its messaging architecture. The goal is quantum-resilient communication that doesn't compromise Threema's operational efficiency or its signature no-phone-number, no-email anonymous model.
