OPENAI & CHATGPT

OpenAI Plans to Nearly Double Its Workforce
OpenAI plans to grow from roughly 4,500 employees to about 8,000 by year-end 2026, with most new hires targeting product development, engineering, research, and sales. The company is also ramping up a new class of "technical ambassadors" to help businesses extract more value from its tools. The move comes as OpenAI faces intensifying competition from Anthropic and Google.

OpenAI announced its acquisition of Astral, a developer tooling startup, to accelerate growth of its Codex platform and power the next generation of Python developer tools. The acquisition signals OpenAI's continued push into the developer workflow space.

OpenAI plans to merge ChatGPT, the Codex coding platform, and its Atlas browser into a single desktop "superapp" to consolidate its fragmented product lineup. CHATGPT ATLAS BROWSER

Atlas Latest: Multi-Account, Smarter Agent Mode
Recent Atlas updates include multi-account support (work/personal/school profiles with separate bookmarks and history), a more persistent Agent Mode that pushes through repetitive tasks like parsing hundreds of emails, and browser memories that learn from your browsing to surface relevant context in future chats. PERPLEXITY & COMET

Comet AI Browser Lands on iPhone
Perplexity's Comet browser is now live on the App Store for iPhone — free, after originally launching at $200/month on desktop. The built-in AI assistant works alongside open pages, summarizing content, answering follow-up questions, and handling multi-step tasks like opening a calendar event, searching LinkedIn, and preparing a brief without switching apps. Research threads sync seamlessly between iOS and desktop.
SOURCE // gHacks · MacRumors · Engadget READ →
Comet Enterprise Launches with CrowdStrike Integration
Perplexity released Comet Enterprise with granular admin controls, managed device deployment, and direct integration with CrowdStrike's Falcon platform for phishing detection and data loss prevention. Early customers include Fortune, AWS, AlixPartners, and Bessemer Venture Partners.
SOURCE // Resultsense READ →
⚠ Privacy Warning
Perplexity collects browsing and search history from Comet to build ad-targeting profiles. There is currently no opt-out available within the app. Factor this into your personal privacy posture.
⚠ Security Notice
Researchers demonstrated Comet could be tricked into a phishing scam in under 4 minutes via prompt injection. Two additional zero-click attacks ("PerplexedBrowser") were also disclosed — Perplexity has since patched both issues.

● SIGNAL
Group Member Labels Now Live
Signal shipped group member labels across Android, iOS, and Desktop. Add a short description of your role in any group chat (e.g., "Referee," "Team Lead," "Admin"). Labels appear next to your profile name and are controlled by group admins via the "Edit Group Info" permission.
SOURCE // Signal GitHub READ →
● SIGNAL
End-to-End Encrypted Backups — Now Generally Available
Signal's secure backup feature is now fully live. Back up all messages and 45 days of media for free, or pay a small monthly fee for 100 GB of full attachment storage. Enable it at Settings → Chats → Backups.
SOURCE // Signal App Store · GitHub READ →
● SIGNAL
Key Transparency — In Development
Signal is actively building Key Transparency — a feature that automatically verifies end-to-end encryption remains intact without requiring users to manually check safety numbers via QR codes. Currently in active development across all platforms.
