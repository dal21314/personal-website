OPENAI & CHATGPT

OpenAI Revamps Shopping in ChatGPT, Drops Instant Checkout
OpenAI has overhauled its commerce experience in ChatGPT today, pivoting away from its failed Instant Checkout feature — which struggled to onboard merchants and handle multi-item carts — toward a product discovery model built on its new Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP). Shoppers can now upload images or describe items and get visual, side-by-side comparisons across retailers. Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Lowe's, Best Buy, and Wayfair are among the first ACP-integrated retailers, and Walmart launched a dedicated in-app ChatGPT experience today with loyalty and payment support. Shopify also announced it is connecting its merchants' storefronts to the catalog for direct checkout via an in-app browser.

OpenAI Foundation Pledges $1 Billion in Grants
The OpenAI Foundation — the nonprofit that ultimately controls OpenAI — pledged today to distribute $1 billion in grants over the next year and scale up its capacity as a philanthropic funder. Board chair Bret Taylor said the goal is to use AI to address humanity's hardest problems and deliver real benefits in people's lives. The move follows an October 2025 regulatory agreement that clarified the nonprofit's ownership stake, valued at $130 billion, making it one of the best-resourced nonprofits in the country. CHATGPT ATLAS BROWSER

Superapp Consolidation: ChatGPT + Codex + Atlas Merging Into One Desktop App
The big strategic story from this week: OpenAI plans to merge ChatGPT, the Codex coding platform, and its Atlas browser into a single desktop "superapp." OpenAI president Greg Brockman is leading the overhaul, while applications chief Fidji Simo will handle distribution. The trigger was honest internal self-assessment: too many parallel products fragmented engineering resources, diluted focus, and — crucially — allowed Anthropic to rapidly close the enterprise gap. The WSJ reported that Anthropic now captures 73% of first-time enterprise AI spending, prompting Simo to declare an internal "code red." The mobile ChatGPT app will remain separate; no launch timeline has been announced for the superapp itself.
SOURCE // The Decoder / MacRumors READ →
Atlas Recent Changelog Highlights
While the superapp is coming, Atlas continues shipping fast. Recent updates include: multi-account support (sign in with separate work, personal, and school ChatGPT profiles each with independent browser history); a less "lazy" Agent Mode that stays persistent through repetitive tasks like bulk email parsing; browser memories that store web context to improve future chat responses; and a new fuzzy-match "Command+F" that finds similar text when exact matches don't exist. Agent Mode is in preview for Plus, Pro, and Business users. By default, OpenAI does not train on your browsing content.
SOURCE // OpenAI Help Center READ →
// Bottom Line
Atlas is macOS-only today, but as the coming superapp takes shape, this is where OpenAI's entire desktop future is headed — one unified agentic workspace for browsing, coding, and chat.

PERPLEXITY & COMET BROWSER

Comet Now Free on All Platforms — iOS, Android, Mac, Windows
Perplexity's Comet browser has completed its cross-platform rollout, landing on iPhone (free on the App Store) after previously launching on Android, Mac, and Windows. The iOS version includes Deep Research, voice mode, page summarization, hybrid search (Google for quick navigational queries, Perplexity for complex research), and multi-step agentic tasks — all without leaving the browser. Research threads sync seamlessly between iPhone and desktop. Apple's platform rules require iOS browsers to use WebKit, so Comet can't use its own Chromium engine on iPhone; extensions are also unsupported on iOS. Comet can be set as the default browser on iPhone.
SOURCE // gHacks / Engadget READ →
Comet Enterprise + Personal Computer — Perplexity Goes Pro and Local
Perplexity this week also shipped Comet Enterprise (MDM-deployable across macOS and Windows, with granular admin controls and CrowdStrike Falcon integration for phishing and DLP) and announced Personal Computer — an always-on AI agent running on a dedicated Mac mini, merging local files, apps, and sessions with Perplexity Computer for 24/7 autonomous task handling. Personal Computer is in early-access waitlist mode. Perplexity Computer — the broader agentic desktop suite — is now available to all Pro subscribers, and also launched in Slack and for Enterprise organizations this week.
SOURCE // Perplexity Changelog READ →
⚠️ Privacy Warning
Perplexity has publicly stated that Comet collects browsing and search history to build ad-targeting profiles. There is currently no opt-out option within the app. The free-tier model directly funds this data collection strategy.
⚠️ Security Notice
Security researchers demonstrated that Comet could be tricked into a phishing scam in under four minutes via prompt injection (intent collision). Trail of Bits also disclosed four injection techniques that exfiltrated Gmail data via the AI assistant. Two additional zero-click attacks (collectively "PerplexedBrowser") involving meeting-invite injection and 1Password hijacking were also disclosed by Zenity Labs. Perplexity has patched all known issues.

ANTHROPIC & CLAUDE

Claude Can Now Control Your Mac — Research Preview Live Today
Anthropic's biggest product news today: Claude can now use your computer to complete tasks end-to-end through Claude Cowork and Claude Code on macOS. When Claude doesn't have a direct connector for an app like Slack or Google Calendar, it falls back to controlling your screen directly — pointing, clicking, navigating your browser, filling spreadsheets, opening files, and running dev tools automatically, with no extra setup. Available as a research preview for Claude Pro and Max subscribers on macOS only; Windows x64 support is planned for a future update. The capability pairs with Dispatch (released last week), which lets you assign tasks from your iPhone and return to finished work on your desktop. Claude will always ask for explicit permission before accessing a new file, folder, or app.
SOURCE // MacRumors / CNBC / 9to5Mac READ →
Claude Code Gets "Auto Mode" — Smarter Permissions Without the Risk
Separately, Anthropic shipped "auto mode" for Claude Code today — available in research preview to Claude Teams users, with Enterprise and API access coming in the next few days. It's a middle path between the default conservative permission model (requiring approval for each file write or bash command) and the blunt "dangerously-skip-permissions" flag. In auto mode, a classifier reviews each tool call before it runs, blocking potentially destructive actions like mass file deletion or data exfiltration, while letting safe operations proceed without interrupting the workflow. Anthropic currently recommends using it in isolated/sandboxed environments. Auto mode works with Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.6.
SOURCE // TechCrunch / 9to5Mac READ →
Anthropic vs. Pentagon: Federal Judge Signals DOD May Have Violated the Law
In a hearing today in San Francisco federal court, U.S. District Judge Rita Lin said the Pentagon's blacklisting of Anthropic's Claude models "looks like an attempt to cripple" the company, and pressed government lawyers on whether the Department of Defense violated the law by designating Anthropic a supply chain risk. The dispute stems from Anthropic's refusal to allow Claude to be used for fully autonomous weapons or mass surveillance of Americans. Trump issued a Truth Social post ordering agencies to immediately cease use of Anthropic's technology after negotiations broke down. Without a preliminary injunction, Anthropic says it could lose billions in government contracts. Judge Lin said she expects to issue an order in the next few days.
SOURCE // CNBC READ →
💡 Note
Computer use is still described as early-stage compared to Claude's coding and text capabilities. Anthropic recommends starting with non-sensitive, routine workflows — file organization, meeting note collation, spreadsheet assembly — before expanding to sensitive data.

SECURE MESSAGING SPOTLIGHT

● SIGNAL
FBI Warns: Russian Hackers Actively Targeting Signal Accounts
The FBI and CISA issued a warning this week about Russian intelligence-affiliated threat actors conducting phishing campaigns targeting Signal (and WhatsApp) accounts of high-value individuals — including current and former U.S. government officials, military personnel, journalists, and political figures. The attacks use social engineering: bad actors impersonate "Signal Support" and trick victims into providing their SMS verification code or scanning a malicious QR code, which links the attacker's device to the victim's account. Signal has never and will never initiate contact via in-app messages or SMS to request verification codes or PINs. If anyone asks for yours — it is a scam. Enable a Registration Lock PIN in Signal Settings → Account → Registration Lock.
SOURCE // The Hacker News READ →
● SIGNAL
Member Labels, Secure Backups GA, and Key Transparency in Progress
Signal's recent feature releases: Group Member Labels are live on Android, iOS, and Desktop, letting users add a short role description (e.g., "Moderator," "Coach," "Lead") that appears next to their profile name in that group — group admins control whether labels are enabled. Secure Backups are now fully live and generally available: back up all messages and the last 45 days of media for free, or pay a small monthly fee for 100 GB to keep every attachment. Enable via Signal Settings → Chats → Backups. Key Transparency — which automatically verifies E2E encryption integrity without manual safety number checks — is still in active development per the Signal GitHub.

● THREEMA
Why There's No Such Thing as a "Threema User Account" — And Why That Matters Right Now
Threema published a notable blog post (March 11) clarifying the key distinction between its model and conventional paid online services. A Swiss email provider recently handed over a user's data — including credit card information — to authorities following a legal request. Threema is also paid software, but users pay for the app itself, not an account. A Threema ID is generated locally from a random finger gesture, with no email, phone number, or billing information tied to it on any server. There is no account to subpoena. Separately, Threema completed its transfer of ownership to German investment firm Comitis Capital in January 2026, which is supporting the company's international expansion with a stated commitment to preserving Threema's privacy values.
SOURCE // Threema Blog / Privacy Guides READ →
// Privacy Note
Threema remains the gold standard for anonymous messaging — no phone number, no email, no server-side account to subpoena. The FBI Signal phishing campaign this week is a reminder that even the best tools require good security hygiene: enable a Registration Lock PIN and never share verification codes.
